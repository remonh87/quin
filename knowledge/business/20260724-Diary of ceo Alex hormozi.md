Another interesting take that they were talking about is about customers. They were saying that nowadays, a lot of companies are just all about hyper growth and acquiring new customers, but what they forget to realize is that it is very costly to acquire new customers. If you lose your existing ones, it means you need to acquire more, which is very expensive, and then the underlying financials of the business are not good.  
  
He had an example. Let's say you have a business that acquires 3 million customers each year but loses them as well. That means after three years, he still ends up with 3 million. If you have a business that acquires new customers of around 1 million each year, it means he will retain them as well and then end up in year three as well with 3 million customers. The underlying financials are much better because they didn't spend money to acquire 9 million new users.

Continuing on serving the wealthier companies or individuals, it's very important to know that you need to price your service properly. One of the best things is to do a Van Westendorp price analysis. What it does is it will actually split your service into a couple of areas:  
1. At what price would the service or product be considered too expensive, and I don't even consider buying it?  
2. Where is it too cheap that I don't consider it valuable?  
3. When do I consider it to be a bargain   
4. when it actually feels premium?
