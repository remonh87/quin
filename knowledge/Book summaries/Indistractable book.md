Worth noting it’s the belief, not just the label.  Ego depletion is the theory that willpower is a finite mental resource, like a muscle that tires with use. The idea holds that exerting self-control on one task leaves less available for the next, so you’re more likely to give in later. It was popularized by Roy Baumeister’s experiments, where subjects who resisted one temptation performed worse on a following self-control task. However, later research challenged it: large replication attempts failed to reproduce the effect reliably. Notably, studies found the depletion mainly appears in people who _believe_ willpower is limited—suggesting it’s driven by mindset, not a fixed fuel tank
